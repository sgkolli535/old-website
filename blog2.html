A Good Hindu

My father always said,

"A good Hindu goes to the temple"

My mother always nodded her head

As she often did in my father's presence

My brother always scoffed at his words

Which our family came to ignore

But I always listened with diligence

Over the years some questions arose

Regarding the definition of a good Hindu

Of the importance of the temple

And the example of a real devotee

Does a good Hindu go to the temple

To see her father put only 50 rupees in the hundi*

Which the neighbors and even the priests jeer at

That later leads him to hang himself out of shame

Does a good Hindu chant hymns at the temple

To see her mother mocked as a widow

By the "pious" women in the sanctuary

That provoked her depression and early death

Does a good Hindu pray at the temple

To see her brother beaten to death on the marble steps

For borrowing 100 rupees to feed his sister

That he repaid a single day late

I have yet to set foot in a temple after my childhood

Some say I am not devout enough

They shun me for my lack of faith

But my choices are within reason

Many do not seem to understand

That devotion is not stealing prasad*

Or throwing wads of cash for donation

Or buying hoards of prayer books

Devotion is my father

When he labored in the mines to buy my school shoes

Devotion is my mother

When she sold her ring for my textbooks

Devotion is my brother

When he started working at 15 years old for my lunches

Everything my family did to raise me

Revealed the true meaning of devotion

Every one of them was a good Hindu

As God does not reside in the temple

He is within the hearts of the true devotees

*hundi- a location in temples in which donations towards the temple are deposited

*prasad- a small offering of food during a religious ritual that is later given to the attendees
